How to Use Comparium

üéØ Quick Start Guide

  1. Select Fish Species: Click on a category to expand it, then click on a fish species to select it. You must select at least 2 fish to compare.
  2. Use Search: Type in the search box to quickly find fish by common or scientific name.
  3. Compare: Click the "Compare Species" button to see detailed compatibility analysis.
  4. Review Results: Check temperature, pH, tank size requirements, and compatibility warnings.
üí° Tip: Start by selecting fish you're interested in, then let Comparium tell you if they can live together safely!

üìä Understanding the Results

What We Compare:

  • Temperature Range: Fish need overlapping temperature requirements to thrive together
  • pH Level: Water acidity/alkalinity must be compatible
  • Water Hardness: Soft vs hard water preferences
  • Tank Size: Minimum gallons needed for all selected species
  • Adult Size: How large each fish will grow
  • Temperament: Peaceful, semi-aggressive, or aggressive behavior
  • Diet: Omnivore, carnivore, or herbivore
  • Social Needs: Schooling requirements and group sizes
  • Lifespan: Expected years with proper care
  • Care Level: Difficulty from "Very Easy" to "Difficult"

üîç Special Features

Predation Detection:

Comparium automatically warns you about:

  • Fish that will eat shrimp or snails (any fish 2+ inches with omnivore/carnivore diet)
  • Size mismatches where tiny fish (<1 inch) could be eaten by larger fish
  • General predation risk when fish are 3x larger than tankmates

Problem Species Warnings:

We flag commonly mis-sold "problem fish" that beginners should avoid or research carefully:

  • Common Pleco: Grows 18-24 inches, needs 75-180+ gallon tanks
  • Clown Loach: Grows 10-12 inches, lives 20+ years, needs 125+ gallons
  • Chinese Algae Eater: Becomes aggressive with age
  • Convict Cichlid: Extreme breeding aggression
  • Paradise Fish: Highly aggressive, species tank recommended
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Comparium is a planning tool, not a guarantee. Always:
  • Research each species thoroughly from multiple sources
  • Consult with experienced aquarists or your local fish store
  • Consider individual fish personalities - some are more aggressive than typical
  • Quarantine new fish before adding to established tanks
  • Monitor your tank closely when introducing new species
